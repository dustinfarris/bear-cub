* Design

Verbatim from DESIGN.org for this story's scope.

** Kiosk routine region — state 2 (Screens)

2. *Complete + window active* (collapsed): banner shows the completion icon, plus its bonus badge unless the bonus is forfeited (D52); the region shows the completion message (=morning_complete/0= / =evening_complete/0=), and *morning additionally reveals extras* (outstanding + done-today, each tap-to-complete / undo — D28, D33). The chore list is hidden. A failed extra here shows a *warning + −N* (its own value); a completed extra here shows a green *+N chip* (its own value) to the left of the checkmark — the positive mirror of the failed extra's −N (D54). (SC-6, SC-7)

** Earned-value marking on the kiosk (Key Behaviors)

The positive mirror of failed-chore marking, at the same per-chore honesty the penalty side observes (D45, D46):

- *Completed extra:* a green *+N chip* (its own =chore.points= value) to the left of the checkmark on the extra card, in the morning reveal (state 2). This is the exact positive mirror of the failed extra's warning + −N.
- *Completed routine chore:* *no per-chore number*. A routine chore earns nothing on its own — the routine pays a single +R no matter how many chores it holds — so a per-chore +5 would misstate the one +R the routine actually pays, the same honesty principle that keeps a failed routine chore's card numberless (D45, D46). The routine-level reward stays the banner completion-icon bonus badge (D51, D52), shown once per routine-day.

The green +N chip is a new kids'-view indicator; per repo convention it is recorded as a design-language ruling (green chip = earned value on extras) in =docs/design-language.org= when the work lands, not at design time — a *pending design-language recording* (the D48 pattern). (SC-2, SC-6, D54)

** Failed-chore marking on the kiosk (Key Behaviors) — mirror context, untouched by this story

- *Failed extra:* a warning icon *+ −N* (its own =chore.points= value) on the extra card, in the morning reveal (state 2).
- *Failed routine chore:* a warning icon on the card (signals "redo me"), *no per-chore number*.

** Decision Log entry in scope

- *D54 (2026-07-17) — Kiosk earned badge: a completed extra shows a green +N chip; completed routine chores show no per-chore number:* a completed (done-today) extra's card shows a green *+N chip* (its own =chore.points= value) to the left of the checkmark — the positive mirror of D46's failed-extra −N — while a completed routine chore shows *no per-chore number*, because a routine earns a single +R no matter how many chores it holds; a per-chore +5 would misstate the one +R the routine pays, the same per-chore-honesty principle as D45. The routine-level reward stays the Story 08 banner completion-icon bonus badge (D51, D52). The green chip is a new kids'-view indicator, recorded as a design-language ruling (green chip = earned value on extras) in =docs/design-language.org= by the story that lands it, per the D48 pattern — a *pending design-language recording*, not written at design time. Rejected — a badge on every completed chore card (routine chores included): would misrepresent routine earning the way a per-chore −N misrepresents the capped penalty (D46), breaking the honesty the penalty side already observes. Settles the SC-2 / SC-6 earned-marking mirror. → Screens (routine region), Key Behaviors (earned-value marking), Overview.

* User Story

As a kid, I want to see how many points an extra earned me right on its card when I finish it — a green +N next to the checkmark — so that finishing an extra feels rewarding, while my routine chores stay clean of per-chore numbers because the routine pays one bonus, not a number per chore.
