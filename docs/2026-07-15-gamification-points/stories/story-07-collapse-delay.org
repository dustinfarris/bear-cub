** Batch member — collapse-delay (Overview)

Two oneshots from the same completion-reward theme are claimed as batch members of this initiative (PRD SC-7): the *collapse-delay* (the brief per-chore-completion pause before the list collapses) and the *big-icon* completion affordance.

** Completion celebration — collapse-delay (Key Behaviors)

Routine completion is celebrated on the kiosk (SC-7):

- *Collapse-delay:* a short delay between the last routine chore completing and the routine list collapsing, so the child sees the per-chore completion reward before the whole-routine collapse/bonus moment. (Batch member; SC-7 "each chore's completion is briefly visible before the routine list collapses.")

* User Story

As a kid, I need a brief moment to see my last chore turn "done" before the whole routine collapses, so that finishing the last task feels like its own small reward before the finished-the-routine celebration.

* Technical Notes

The Design excerpt above is the source of truth; DESIGN.org is broader context.

- One of the two claimed batch members (collapse-delay). Independent of the other stories in ordering, but placed here so it lands alongside the completion celebration work. Depends on the existing collapse mechanic (Extras Story 05).
- The design deliberately specifies "a short delay" at the design level — the concrete duration is implementation freedom, tuned at the on-device gate. Write the test at that specificity: assert the last-chore-completion is visible before the collapse, not a specific millisecond value.
- No gaps surfaced — the "short delay" imprecision is intentional per the initiative's guidance (design-level, tuned at the on-device gate), not an uncovered detail.
- The on-device feel (the actual pause length in Fully Kiosk's WebView) is verified at the manual batch-close gate, not by a per-story AC.
- Deferred discovery: a rapid undo/redo of the last chore within the ~@collapse_delay_ms~ (400ms) window can schedule more than one ~{:collapse_ready, kid_id}~ timer for the same kid (the first timer ref is never stored/cancelled on undo). The extra message is a harmless no-op (~MapSet.delete~ on an already-absent key plus an idempotent reload), so no incorrect UI state results, but a very fast undo->redo could make the band appear slightly earlier than a full fresh delay after the final completion. Not covered by any AC — the design's "short delay" is explicitly imprecise at this level — surfaced for ~docs/backlog.org~ via the initiative's PLAN Deferred section, not fixed inline.
