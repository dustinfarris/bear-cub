** Batch member — collapse-delay (Overview)

Two oneshots from the same completion-reward theme are claimed as batch members of this initiative (PRD SC-7): the *collapse-delay* (the brief per-chore-completion pause before the list collapses) and the *big-icon* completion affordance.

** Completion celebration — collapse-delay (Key Behaviors)

Routine completion is celebrated on the kiosk (SC-7):

- *Collapse-delay:* a short delay between the last routine chore completing and the routine list collapsing, so the child sees the per-chore completion reward before the whole-routine collapse/bonus moment. (Batch member; SC-7 "each chore's completion is briefly visible before the routine list collapses.")

* User Story

As a kid, I need a brief moment to see my last chore turn "done" before the whole routine collapses, so that finishing the last task feels like its own small reward before the finished-the-routine celebration.

* Acceptance Criteria

** [ ] When the last routine chore is completed, its completion is briefly visible before the routine list collapses — a short delay separates the last-chore-done moment from the collapse/bonus moment. (SC-7)
** [ ] After the short delay, the routine list collapses to its completed state as before; completing a chore that is *not* the last one causes no delay. (SC-7)
